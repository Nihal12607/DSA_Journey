Stack: Most operations like push, pop, and top operate in constant time, O(1) 

Queue: Similar to stack, operations such as push, pop, and accessing the front or back elements occur in constant time, O(1)

Priority Queue:
push and pop operations occur in logarithmic time, O(log n) .
Accessing the top element happens in constant time, O(1).

Set:
Operations like insert, erase, and find occur in logarithmic time, O(log n) .

Unordered Set:
Most operations (insert, erase, find) occur in constant time, O(1) on average .
In the rare worst-case scenario, it can degrade to linear time, O(n) .

Map:
Operations like insert, erase, and find typically take logarithmic time, O(log n) .

Unordered Map:
Generally operates in constant time, O(1) .
Like the unordered set, it can degrade to linear time, O(n) in the worst case
